Founded in 2012, by Mama Betty, together with other like-minded persons, Juba Rescue Centre in Juba South Sudan was preceded by initial visits to street children and families and later to prisons where young children whose parents were in custody did not have access to basic supplies.Juba Rescue Centre was initially founded and widely known as St.Clare House.

With the support of friends an some personal donations, this started as single meals on the street in a day and later some clothes, treatment and medicine for many who had simple infections such as common cold, coughing and malaria, where others were referred to hospitals for further management.Over the years, it has grown into a place of stability and hope, supporting children through education, healthcare, emotional support, and a loving community.

To date, St Clare Rescue House regularly receives calls from a vast number of police stations across Juba including but not limited to Jebel, Munuki, Juba Town, Malakia and Referendum among others.

From these police stations, the home has gained confidence due to the ability to quickly react and pick up children who are often reported as abandoned at one to five days upon delivery Others are a few months old. Upon collection, the infants usually require an immediate comprehensive medical assessment as well as some basic supplies including infant baby milk, clothes and pampers. Upon arrival at the centre, they are allocated a fulltime caretaker or babysitter responsible for them day and night until they attain the first few milestones such as being able to be weaned off feeding and able to feed themselves by hand.

The Rescue Centre has also become home to young girls fleeing or rescued from abuse and early forced marriage situations which is a rampant traditional in South Sudan.

Due to this, we have taken in children who are either expectant or nursing babies out of either forced unions or defilement by their caregivers.

Our Mission

To welcome, nurture and build abandoned, orphaned and abused children by providing stable family-style living arrangements that center on their physical, emotional and spiritual growththrough healthcare, education and life-skills based on strong community partnerships.

Our Vision

To become a model institution that helps vulnerable children through effective approaches and programs that form a foundation for a bright, independent future aimed at successful community integration.
