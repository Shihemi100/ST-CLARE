Early Childhood Learning

Children between the ages of two and eight receive early learning support within the centre through educational activities and basic classroom instruction.

This has been made possible donations of learning materials and the support of dedicated voluntee teachers who spend time nurturing and engaging with the children.

Through these efforts, the centre continues to create a supportive learning environment that encourages growth, confidence and development during the children's early years.

Primary & Secondary Education

Children at the centre attend nearby local primary schools within walking distance, allowing them to remain connected to the surrounding community while accessing basic education.

About twenty older children are also supported through partnerships that help sponsor their education at institutions such as Don Bosco School, Kit; wa boarding primary and secodary school.

Depending on their educational pathway, some students sit for the Kenyan national examination known as the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education(KCPE); this is benefial as it enables children to apply for opportunities internationally. Other children undertake South Sudan's local natonal examinations.

Due to challenges such as age and the lack of a permanent home for the centre, some children are unfortunately forced to completely drop out of school or relocate before completing their education. Despite these difficulties, the centre continues to encourage and support every child's educational journey wherever possible.

Higher Learning & Mentorship

Due to limited resources and lack of consistent support for education beyond the basic level, many young people at the centre are unable to access higher education opportunities. As a result, vocational training has become an important pathway toward independence and self-reliance.

Over the years, a few children have been supported in voactional programs such as computer studies, tailoring and secretarial training after completing secondary education. There have been efforts to acquire tailoring machines and other learning equipment so that vocationol skills training can be offered directly within the centre.

These practical skills aim at providing young people with opportunites to earn a living and build more stable futures despite the challenges they face.
